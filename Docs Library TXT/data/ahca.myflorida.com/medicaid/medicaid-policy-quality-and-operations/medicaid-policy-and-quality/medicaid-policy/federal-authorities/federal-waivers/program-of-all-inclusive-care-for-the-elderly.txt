Florida Medicaid’s Covered Services and HCBS Waivers
Program  of All-Inclusive Care for the Elderly
Program of All-inclusive Care for the Elderly (PACE) organizations provide primary care, social,  restorative, and supportive services for Medicaid and Medicare eligible  individuals 55 years of age and older who live in a PACE service area. PACE  organizations provide care and services in the home, the community, the nursing  facility, and in the PACE center.  All  Medicare and Medicaid services must be available, including:

Acute care services
Adult day health care
End-of life  care services
Home care
Home-delivered or congregate meals
Hospital care
Laboratory  tests, x-rays and other diagnostic procedures
Medical  specialty services
Medical  supplies and appliances
Nursing facility care
Nutritional counseling
Personal care
Prescription drugs
Primary  care services (including physician and nursing)
Recreational  therapy
Restorative  therapies
Social  Services
Therapy services
Transportation

Program of All-inclusive Care for the Elderly is not offered by health  plans serving Medicaid enrollees.
Eligibility
Medicaid recipients  and/or Medicare beneficiaries who meet the following criteria are eligible to  enroll in the Program of All-inclusive Care for the Elderly:

Fifty-five  years of age or older
At risk  of institutionalization
Live in  the designated Program of All-inclusive Care for the Elderly service area.
Able to live safely in the community

Resource Information
More information on the Florida PACE program is  available on the Florida  Department of Elder Affairs webpage.
