June 2015: Request for Renewal of MEDS-AD 1115 Research and Demonstration Waiver











June 2015: Request for Renewal of MEDS-AD 1115 Research and Demonstration WaiverThe Agency is seeking to extend the federal waiver authority to continue providing Medicaid eligibility to the MEDS-AD group, according to provisions of Section 409.904(1), Florida Statutes, which states:Subject to federal waiver approval, a person who is age 65 or older or is determined to be disabled, whose income is at or below 88 percent of the federal poverty level, whose assets do not exceed established limitations, and who is not eligible for Medicare or, if eligible for Medicare, is also eligible for and receiving Medicaid-covered institutional care services, hospice services, or home and community-based services. The agency shall seek federal authorization through a waiver to provide this coverage.Since January 2006, Medicaid eligibility for this group has been authorized through the Florida Medicaid MEDS-AD 1115 Research and Demonstration Waiver #11-W-00205/4. The current federal approval for this waiver will expire on December 31, 2015. Florida Medicaid is requesting a renewal of authority to continue the program as it currently operates through December 31, 2018.Renewal Request SubmissionOn June 30, 2015, the Agency plans to submit the 3-year waiver renewal request to the CMS. Public InputThe Agency must allow a 30-day public notice and comment period regarding renewal applications for a demonstration project that the Agency plans to submit to CMS for review and consideration. The Agency will consider the public comments while developing the renewal application for the MEDS-AD 1115 Research and Demonstration Waiver.
