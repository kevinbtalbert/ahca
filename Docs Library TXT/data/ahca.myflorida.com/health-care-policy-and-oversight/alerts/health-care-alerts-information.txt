Health Care Alerts Information











Health Care ProvidersReceive updates that impact health care providers or suppliers regulated by the Agency. Examples of updates include legislative changes, rulemaking activities, and regulatory updates.Managed Care ProvidersReceive updates that impact managed care providers regulated by the Agency. Examples of updates include legislative changes, rulemaking activities, and regulatory updates.Office of Plans and Construction / Life Safety InformationReceive updates on any of the selected health care facility types the Agency reviews and approves construction plans. Examples include information about rulemaking activities, legislative changes, regulatory updates, application changes, and life safety topics.Health Care TopicsCertificate of Need (CON)Receive fixed need pool notices for upcoming batches (hospital beds, facilities, and other beds and programs), notices of accepted letters of intent and applications, and batching cycle initial Agency decisions. Examples of updates include legislative changes, rulemaking activities, and regulatory updates related to CON.Emergency OrdersReceive notice of legal orders the Agency issues that place temporary restrictions on a facility or provider due to the presence of conditions that constitute an immediate danger to the health, safety and welfare of patients or residents at that facility. The temporary restrictions may be lifted once the facility or provider has taken the necessary steps to eliminate the presence of those conditions, although further disciplinary action may be taken by the Agency.Florida Health Information Exchange (HIE)Receive updates from the Florida Health Information Exchange, which provides services to facilitate the secure exchange of health information across many facets of the health care industry. Examples of updates include upcoming webinars on Florida HIE services, News Bytes (a newsletter on Health IT), and Health IT events across the state.Health AlertsReceive general health alerts from the Agency and organizations the Agency works with such as the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, the Food and Drug Administration, the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services, and the Department of Health.TelehealthReceive information and updates related to the Telehealth Advisory Council. Examples of updates include meeting announcements, media alerts, and publications.
